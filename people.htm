Curriculum Vitae (pdf)

Dr. Chongzhi Zang is an Associate Professor in the Department of Genome Sciences at the University of Virginia and serves as the Director of Computational Genomics at the UVA Comprehensive Cancer Center. He is also affiliated with the Departments of Biochemistry and Molecular Genetics, and Biomedical Engineering. Chongzhi received his BS in physics from Peking University and PhD in physics from the George Washington University. He did his undergraduate research in high-field laser physics with Jie Zhang at the Institute of Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences, and his PhD research in computational biophysics with Weiqun Peng and Keji Zhao at the National Institutes of Health. Before joining UVA in November 2016, he completed his postdoctoral training with Xiaole Shirley Liu at Harvard University's Dana-Farber Cancer Institute. Chongzhi is a computational biologist with expertise in epigenetics and transcriptional regulation. His research focuses on developing computational methods for high-throughput omics data analysis and integrative modeling of gene regulation in mammalian cell systems.
